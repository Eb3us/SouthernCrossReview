Socially Distant Love


It's more I'd need than sight unseen
My sins to socially redeem.

Even thoughts of places where
We'd been together could not care.

To feel your searching finger-tips,
Your breast impulsing in my lips:

Even these are not enough,
Are cool, yes, but rude and rough

Without your heart free to feel
My love, though distant, surely real.

And dauntless despite its dreary depth
In proud, never distant death.

What is life? I ask you now.
To answer means to realize how

Without love comprising all,
Without death's final call,

It's all no more than meaningless
A shambles that God refused to bless.

FTS, July 2021
